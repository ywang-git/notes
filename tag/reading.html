14 How to Read Imaginative Literature

strange paradox

belief

  • knowing how to read imaginative literature is much more difficult than the problem of knowing how to read expository books
  • sees such skill is more widely possessed than the art of reading science and philosophy, politics, economics and history

symptom

  • easier to tell whether to like a fictional work / hard to reason why
  • good readers but not good critics

reasoning

  • to be good critic, has to apprehend the novel
  • imaginative literature pleases rather than teach, so it is easier to be pleased than taught, beauty is harder to analyze than truth

How Not to Read Imaginative Literature

basic differences between expository and imaginative literature

purpose

  • expository books: try to convey knowledge

  • imaginative: try to communicate an experience

  • to experience: through the exercise of sense and imagination

  • to know: use power of judgement and reasoning (intellectual) does not mean these are separate
